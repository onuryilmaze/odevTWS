BIO

Haruki Murakami, born on January 12, 1949, in Kyoto, Japan, is a renowned Japanese author. He is known for exploring themes of modern society, loneliness, and the search for identity in his works. His first novel, Hear the Wind Sing, was published in 1979, but he gained significant recognition with Norwegian Wood in 1987. Other notable works include 1Q84, Kafka on the Shore, and The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle. Murakami's books have attracted a wide readership globally.

FAMILY

Haruki Murakami married Yoko Murakami in 1971. Yoko Murakami is a well-known translator and writer in Japan. The couple has one daughter. Yoko has played a significant role in translating and editing many of Haruki’s works. The Murakami family generally keeps their private lives away from the media, maintaining their privacy. The balance between Haruki Murakami's work and family life is considered an important aspect of his creative process.

N-WOOD

Norwegian Wood is Haruki Murakami’s best-selling book. Published in 1987, it achieved significant commercial success. The book enhanced Murakami’s international recognition and introduced Japanese culture to a wider audience. Critics praised the novel for its character depth and emotional intensity. Readers appreciated its emotional narrative, though some criticized its melancholic tone.
